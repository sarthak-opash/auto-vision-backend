01Problem Statement

The Insurance Claims Crisis

The global auto insurance industry processes over 250 million claims per year. The traditional process is deeply flawed:

  • A customer files a claim after an accident — form-filling alone takes 30–90 minutes.
  • A field assessor is dispatched physically — typical wait: 3–7 business days.
  • The assessor manually photographs damage and writes a report — highly subjective, varies by experience.
  • Repair shop quote frequently conflicts with insurer's estimate — disputes delay settlement.
  • Total claims settlement: 2–8 weeks, causing major customer dissatisfaction and churn.

Key Pain Points by Stakeholder

Insurance CompanyInconsistent damage evaluation standardsFraud leakage: 10–15% of claims

Why Computer Vision is the Right Solution

Modern deep learning models trained on automotive damage datasets achieve human-level accuracy (>90% mAP) on damage detection tasks. With smartphone ubiquity, high-quality photos are always available. A well-engineered CV pipeline can process a 6-image inspection set in under 3 seconds — replacing a process that currently takes days.

Three Operating Modes

  • Self-Service Customer Mode — Customer uploads photos via mobile-friendly web app. Receives instant preliminary report.
  • Assessor Assisted Mode — Insurance assessor reviews and overrides AI estimates. Final report is co-authored by AI + human.
  • Batch / Fleet Mode — Fleet operators upload bulk vehicle photos via API. Receive aggregated damage reports.

03Key Features

AI/ML Features

  • Multi-angle damage detection using YOLOv8 with ensemble voting across uploaded images
  • Instance segmentation of damaged regions — pixel-precise masks per damage zone
  • 30+ car part labels: hood, windshield, door FL/FR/RL/RR, bumper front/rear, fender, trunk, roof, side mirror, headlight, taillight, wheel, underbody
  • 8 damage type classes: Scratch, Dent, Crack, Shatter, Deformation, Corrosion, Paint Damage, Missing Part
  • 4-level severity scoring: Minor / Moderate / Severe / Total Loss with confidence score
  • Fraud detection signal — flags inconsistencies vs claimed incident type
  • OCR-based license plate extraction for automatic claim linking
  • Vehicle make/model/color recognition to auto-fill claim forms

Platform Features

  • React dashboard with real-time claim tracking via WebSocket
  • Role-based access control: Customer / Assessor / Admin / Fraud Analyst
  • Auto-generated PDF claim report with annotated damage images
  • Analytics dashboard: claim trends, cost heatmaps, model performance metrics
  • REST API with JWT auth — ready for third-party insurance system integration
  • Docker Compose one-command deployment for demo and production

Augmentation Pipeline

  • Mosaic augmentation — 4-image composite, improves detection of small damage regions
  • Random horizontal flip — vehicle damage can appear on any side
  • HSV color jitter (hue ±0.015, sat ±0.7, val ±0.4) — handles different lighting conditions
  • Rain/fog overlay — simulates post-accident weather conditions
  • Gaussian blur (σ 0.1–2.0) — simulates camera shake and out-of-focus shots
  • JPEG compression (quality 50–95) — simulates WhatsApp-compressed photos
  • Random scale (0.5–1.5×) — handles varying camera distances

Cost Estimation Engine

A two-stage hybrid approach: rule-based base costs for explainability, then XGBoost refinement for accuracy.

XGBoost Refinement Features

  • Damaged area in cm² (from segmentation mask + focal length estimation)
  • Damage density score (overlapping damage zones)
  • Vehicle age and model tier (luxury vs economy)
  • City / region code (labor cost index)
  • Historical claim data for similar damage patterns
  • Assessor override history for similar cases (active feedback loop)

Fraud Detection Signal

  • Damage pattern inconsistency — claimed "rear collision" but damage is on front
  • Temporal anomaly — claim filed within 24 hours of policy purchase
  • Prior claim frequency — same customer filed >3 claims in 12 months
  • Damage age estimation — paint oxidation and rust indicators from image features
  • Repair shop anomaly — shop has statistical outlier claim approval rate
  • Cost inflation signal — estimate is >2σ above median for similar damage
⚠️ Ethics Note

The fraud score is a signal for human review — NOT an automated rejection trigger. All flagged claims must be reviewed by a fraud analyst. The score is logged, auditable, and never exposed to the customer.

Custom Dataset Collection Protocol

  1. Photograph at service centers, parking lots, insurance offices (with permission). Target Indian models: Maruti Alto/Swift, Hyundai i20/Creta, Tata Nexon, Mahindra Scorpio, Honda City.
  2. Capture all 8 damage types. For rare types (Deformation, Corrosion), coordinate with a local body shop.
  3. For each vehicle, capture all 6 standard angles: Front, Rear, Left Side, Right Side, Top-Front diagonal, Close-up of damage.
  4. Record metadata per image: make, model, year, damage type, severity (assessor-labeled), city — stored in CSV.
  5. Target: 400 images with minimum 50 instances per damage class before training begins.
  6. Inter-annotator agreement check: 20% of images annotated by two interns. Flag IoU disagreement > 0.2 for review.
